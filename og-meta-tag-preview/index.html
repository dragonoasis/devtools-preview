What Is an OG Meta Tag Preview Tool?

Open Graph (OG) meta tags are HTML meta elements that control how your web pages appear when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and messaging apps. They define the title, description, image, and URL displayed in link previews. This tool lets you input your OG meta tag values and instantly preview how your page will look in Google search results, Facebook/LinkedIn share cards, and Twitter cards — all without publishing or deploying your page. Getting these previews right is essential for maximizing click-through rates and ensuring your content looks professional when shared.

How to Use the OG Meta Tag Preview

  1. Enter your page title in the 'og:title' field. Keep it under 60 characters for best display across all platforms.
  2. Add your page URL in the 'og:url' field. This is the canonical URL that will be shown in previews.
  3. Write a compelling description in the 'og:description' field. Aim for 155 characters or fewer for optimal display.
  4. Paste your OG image URL in the 'og:image' field. The recommended size is 1200×630 pixels for best results across platforms.
  5. Review the live previews below to see how your page will appear on Google, Facebook/LinkedIn, and Twitter.

FAQ

What is the recommended image size for OG meta tags?
The recommended OG image size is 1200×630 pixels with a 1.91:1 aspect ratio. This size works well across Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and most other platforms. Use high-quality images and keep important content centered, as some platforms may crop the edges.
What is the difference between OG tags and Twitter Cards?
Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description, og:image) are used by Facebook, LinkedIn, and many other platforms. Twitter has its own meta tags (twitter:card, twitter:title, twitter:description, twitter:image) but will fall back to OG tags if Twitter-specific tags are not present.
Why does my shared link show the wrong image or title?
Social platforms cache link previews. After updating your OG tags, you need to clear the cache using platform-specific tools: Facebook's Sharing Debugger, Twitter's Card Validator, or LinkedIn's Post Inspector. Changes may take a few minutes to propagate.
